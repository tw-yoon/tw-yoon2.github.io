Logo Redesign

Ever since I joined Northwestern's Korean American Student Association (KASA) last year as a freshman, I wanted to redesign the logo of the club. I liked how the club had a new concept and new logo every year, but I thought the previous logo did not represent the club and promote Korean culture well enough.

This is the new logo for the club. The final design of the logo infuses typography: the logo can be read in both English and Korean. If you look on the left side of each letter (and if you can read Korean), it says “KASA” in Korean (카사). It’s a subtle detail that might be hard to catch, but that is the main design aspect and what I like most about the logo. The colors are from the Korean flag - red, blue, and black on white background. The letter “S” is modified to symbolize the "Taegeuk" pattern shown in the center of the Korean flag. Character Design

In KASA, we have three “families,” or small in-club groups with names based on ancient Korean countries: Baekje, Silla and Goguryeo. As the publicity chair, I thought that the best way to increase club participation was by designing cute little characters for these groups. But I didn't want them to just be cute; I wanted them to also have a cultural contribution for the club.

These are the three characters that represent the club: Bingsoo for Baekje, Saida for Silla, and Goguma for Goguryeo. They are named after Korean food that start with the same initials as the groups. The colors yellow, blue, and red are from the flag colors of these ancient countries, and the fancy ornaments they have on their heads are famous artifacts from their motif country.

They went through a huge facelift - if you see the very first designs of the characters you won’t even recognize them. I had weekly meetings with the club presidents to confirm the design throughout summer break.






Brand Design

Now that I have a logo and characters, what to do next? The logos and characters were used for all of the posts that got uploaded to the KASA Instagram page. Family event posts have their respective character in the post, and official club announcements have a uniformed format with the same font, same background. The motif for the background for club announcement posts were luxury brands, such as Louis Vuitton and Fendi , having repeated patterns of their logo as a design aspect.
