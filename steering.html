Steering Columns & Pillow Blocks

Role: Subteam Member

Date: 2022.Sep - 2023.Apr



Project Summary

The Northwestern Formula SAE team builds a new car every year.I was assigned to the suspension team, and was in charge of the steering system along with two other senior members. Manufacturing the steering columns and pillow blocks were my first individual project for the team.

The goal of this project was to ensure that the steering columns and pillow blocks were designed and fabricated to exact standards so that the parts fit perfectly into the vehicle's cockpit. Through this project, I aimed to establish a foundation for engineering with precision and collaborative problem-solving. As a team, we set a goal to improve from last year’s design, focusing on removing play in the steering.

I started by accurately measuring the angle and distance of the steering columns to ensure a seamless fit within the cockpit's limited space. In order to do so, we as a team chose to use a different u-joint that has a bigger angle mobility compared to last year. The pillow blocks were also redesigned; the new design has a 33% reduced weight compared to last year’s design. Following this, I carefully manufactured the parts using a lathe and a mill to machine the pieces to the required specifications.

Through rigorous measurement and precise machining, we achieved an optimal fit within the cockpit, ensuring both functionality and ergonomic comfort for the driver. All of the components in the steering system were welded together, to ensure that there was no play in the system. If I were to redo the project again, I would like to manufacture the pillow blocks using the CNC mill for precision and a more optimal design.
